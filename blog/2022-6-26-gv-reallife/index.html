Comparing Go Vacation to real life

For a while now, I have been obsessing over my favorite Wii game, Go Vacation. I realized that it is better than real life and it has opened my eyes to some ways in which real life is so deprived.

We could be building cities better

Not enough real-life cities are like Kawawii City Resort, and suburbia is too prevalent and too lifeless.

There is this new retail area being built in my area. It looks nice and is coming along nicely, but I can't help but notice all the space they are wasting building so much flat land with nothing but painted lines for infrastructure that only supports giant pain machines. Worse, skateboards and bikes are against the rules there.

Why not build an obstacle course for skaters there instead of a parking lot? Grab some air and bust a move! We need more people out there doing cool tricks, and infrastructure that supports fun! Maybe in all those wide roads too! Plenty of potential wiggle room for roller blades, bikes, or skateboards!

"But where will all the cars go?" In Kawawii City Resort, there is not a single car or car parking in sight! (outside of motor fest) It's walkable, and one can fly around on roller blades or a skateboard. Too tired for that, need a lift, or just want to relax on the way? Then hop on the trolley! Why drive hundreds of automotives around where a few trolleys will suffice?

Gee, it sure is boring around here

Having all those activities in that game made me realize how much we are lacking in fun activities in many real world places.

There isn't much to do in the suburbs. It's all just big box shopping, fa(s)t food places, and gas stations. I say let's demolish all that and build a Sidewinder!

ok maybe that's too dangerous to build irl. Let's play dodgeball with pies! (ones that aren't edible as to not waste food)

It's hard to find interesting things to do when most of what's around is just shopping and feeding consumerism. For the last several decades, people were all like "buy buy buy buy buy buy buy buy" that we really only thought about building shops. I guess more people are stopping because they have to with all the increased inflation and poverty, and maybe it's leading to more consideration for what we could be doing instead of feeding the beast?

How much do we need portable devices?

To vacation in Disney World last year, I needed to register an account on a mobile app for my Magic Band (an RFID wristband used to enter the parks and claim on-ride photos) and to scan QR codes to access all the menus in all the restaurants. On the way there, a mobile app was needed to get airline tickets. My high school's counseling office required me to scan a QR code to fill out a Google Form just to request a schedule change. To get tested for COVID-19 last year, we needed some app just to interact with the doctors. Is this what it takes to set up a new home appliance?

In Kawawii Island, all that's needed to do anything is your person. Just interacting face-to-face with people to enter the activities, change clothes, get information, customize gear, learn tricks, go to different resorts, etc. It's magical. Why can't it be this way in real life? Much like money and automobiles, smartphones weren't necessary until we centered society around them. Thanks a lot Henry Ford and Steve Jobs for propagating such community destroyers!

We don't have enough time off

We have the technology to automate all the jobs and have time off, but we still have to keep working because we need some way to pay for everything because capitalism and the economy and stuff, and it's getting worse.

On this island, everyone, including the locals, are seen enjoying their time here.
